WHY EGO HAS NO FACE

        
Ego is the anyman: the person on the other end of every attack. Not the hacker, the
          human the hacker is counting on. The face is left blank on purpose, because the moment you
          can picture a specific victim, you have already told yourself "that would not be me." Social
          engineering works precisely on the people who are sure they are too smart to fall for it.

        
The name is the point. The flaw an attacker reaches for is not your firewall, it is your
          ego: the part of you that wants to be helpful, to be trusted, to be right, to act
          fast, to not look foolish in front of the boss. Those instincts are good. An attacker simply
          aims them at the wrong target, a fake invoice, a spoofed colleague, an urgent "IT notice,"
          and lets your own helpfulness do the work.

        
So the defense is not a gadget, it is a pause. Before you click, reply, approve, or pay,
          check your ego. Ask whether this message is working on your pride, your fear, or your
          urge to be useful, and slow down exactly when it tells you to hurry.

      


      

        
HEAD TO TOE, CHECK AS YOU GO

        
It is the same move a medic makes on a patient: start at the top and work down, missing
          nothing. A message has a top and a bottom too, so read it that way. Start at the
          head (who it is really from), work down through the body (the links and where
          they actually point), and finish at the toe (the thing it is asking you to do).
          Check each part as you go, and the trick almost always shows itself before you reach the
          bottom. The last stop is the one people skip: your own ego.

      


      

        

          check your ego: head to toe, check as you go

        
head · the senderTop of the message: do you actually know them, or just recognize the name? Look at the real address, not the display name.

        
body · the linkHover before you click. Does the domain match, letter for letter, the place it claims to be?

        
toe · the askThe bottom line: urgency, secrecy, money, or credentials. The more it rushes or flatters you, the more it deserves a second look.

        
last · your egoIs this message working on your pride, your fear, or your wish to help? That feeling is the attack. Check it.
